The Code of Hammurabi was JUST because…

The code was just because one of the laws says that if a free man has knocked out the eye of a free man, then his eye shall be knocked out, too. This is fair because if a free man did it to another free man, then it wouldn't be equal, but they are both free men. So if both of their eyes were knocked out, then it would be fair for both of them.

Another fair law is if a man strikes the daughter of a free man and causes her to lose the fruit of her womb, the man shall pay 10 shekels of silver. This is fair because if you caused harm to the daughter of a free man, then he shall have a punishment, which is 10 shekels of silver. That is an equal law for both of the free men.

And if a man has flooded his neighbor's field, then he has to repair what he has done. This is a fair law because the field is flooded, so he should do the repairs, meaning that he would fix the thing he did wrong.

The Code of Hammurabi was NOT JUST because…

The code was not just because, for example, one of the laws says that if two people are caught being a couple, the law is that they will be tied up and thrown into the water. This law is unfair because if there are no couples, fewer people will be born, making the people miserable.

Another law is if a person breaks into a house creating a hole, then when he is caught, he will face the penalties of either immediate death or he will be hanged in the hole he made. This penalty has gone too far; it should be just like spending a long time in jail or something like that.

Also, another law says that when a surgeon operates with a bronze lancet on a free man for a serious injury and has caused his death, the surgeon's hands will be cut off. This isn't fair because if the man dies, it is not the surgeon's fault that the man died. It should not be this way; the surgeon should not receive any punishment for a man that requires special treatment.
